Service

In high school, I co-founded the computer science club, where our main service was to visit local elementary schools to teach kids how to code.
In our organization we went to different schools weekly to try and excite young children about computer science. This experience was very fulfilling and I wish to do something like this in the future. I was a part of many other service organizations, including the habitat club, National Honor Society, National Technical Honor Society, Spanish National Honor Society, National English Honor Society, National Art Honor Society, Mu Alpha Theta, and National Science Honor Society.
These organizations emphasize community service that allowed me to volunteer at local animal shelters, public libraries and many other
local organizations.

In 2017 I collectively had 100+ hours volunteering during Hurricane Harvey, in which I participated in the destruction and rebuilding of homes affected by flood waters. A group of friends and I went to the neighborhoods that were most affected by the flooding, and helped with damage control.
Here we tried to salvage any furniture or belongings, as well as destroy parts of the house that were getting moldy.
In conjunction with Harvey cleanup, I also volunteered at the local animal shelter, Bayou Animal Services.

Many animals were surrendered during the hurricane, and as a result the shelter was overwhelmed with animals and understaffed. My position
volunteering at the shelter was to help out anyway I could, whether it was bathing dogs or cleaning cages, I helped anyway I could.

When I got to college, I still wanted to regularly volunteer, so I decided to start with the Bryan Animal Center.
I volunteered around 5-10 hours a week, lending a hand wherever needed. I mostly walked the dogs, cleaned cages, and assisted with
the adoption process. While I kept a busy school schedule, volunteering helped me feel balanced and fulfilled.

As a member of a sorority, a large part of our organization is dedicated to philanthropy. Our philanthropy is dedicated to fundraising
for St. Judes Children's Research Hospital as well as LLS research.
